# Problem Statement — Shared Evaluation Scorecard

## Context

Organizations regularly assess third-party vendors — software tools, IT service providers, consultants — as part of procurement. These decisions involve multiple departments and carry significant financial and operational risk.

The current process is broken.

---

## Users

| User | Role in Evaluation |
|---|---|
| Procurement Manager | Initiates evaluation, collects inputs, makes final decision |
| Legal Team | Evaluates compliance, contract terms, liability |
| Security Team | Evaluates data protection, access controls, certifications |
| Finance | Evaluates cost, ROI, budget fit |
| Department Heads / Operations | Evaluate business fit, workflow compatibility |

---

## Current Challenges

**How it works today:**
1. Procurement manager initiates vendor evaluation
2. Sends spreadsheets or emails to 5-10 stakeholders
3. Each stakeholder evaluates vendors individually, in isolation
4. Stakeholders send back inputs at different times, in different formats
5. Procurement manager manually compiles everything
6. Inconsistent criteria, delays, and errors surface
7. Misalignment between stakeholders discovered late — often after significant time invested
8. Suboptimal or delayed final decision

**Core pain points:**
- No shared criteria — every stakeholder evaluates on their own terms
- No visibility into progress — procurement manager cannot see who has responded
- Misalignment discovered too late — rework required at the final stage
- Manual compilation is error-prone and time-consuming
- No audit trail or structured record of the decision process

---

## What Better Looks Like

- A centralized, structured platform for asynchronous vendor evaluation
- Custom, shared scoring criteria defined once and used by all stakeholders
- Real-time visibility into progress and early misalignment signals
- Time-boxed evaluation cycles with automated reminders and deadline enforcement
- A clean decision summary that can be shared with leadership
- Significantly less manual coordination effort for procurement managers
